Neutron

Like alpha and beta radiation, neutrons are a particulate form of radiation.  However, neutrons are encountered much less commonly than alpha or beta particles.  Typically, they are only found when dealing with nuclear reactors or weapons. 

Unlike the alpha or beta particle, the neutron has no electrical charge.  Neutrons have variable ranges based on the amount of kinetic energy present and how the neutrons were produced.

Neutron radiation is the most penetrating type of particulate ionizing radiation, requiring thick layers of concrete or water for shielding purposes.  This is important for BE when considering appropriate controls to protect personnel.
