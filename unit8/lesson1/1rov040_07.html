X-ray and Gamma

X-ray and gamma radiation produce the highest amount of energy and are referred to as the ionizing portion of the electromagnetic spectrum.

X-ray and gamma radiation is indirectly ionizing, which means that any damage sustained comes from by-products (e.g. free radicals) of the exposure caused when the water of the body or of a cell absorbs the radiation.  Potential health effects associated with x-ray exposure could include cell membrane disruption or damage to cell performance.
