Gamma

Gamma radiation is similar to x-rays because it has no mass and no charge, and travels at the speed of light.  Unlike x-rays, which are emitted from the electron cloud of an atom, gamma rays are emitted from the nucleus and result when the nucleus transitions from a higher to lower energy state. 

Although x-rays and gamma rays are less ionizing than alpha, beta, and neutron particles, they have greater penetrability than alpha and beta radiation.  The most appropriate shielding material for gamma radiation is typically lead.
