Scenario Information

Once you have performed the first two steps of the Occupational and Environmental Health Site Assessment (OEHSA) process, you visit each potential site to conduct Site Reconnaissance and confirm the findings from the Pre-deployment and Baseline Activities.  During your visits, you learn some basic information about the sites by conducting interviews and making observations:

  • You observe stained soil at Site A, indicating potential flightline runoff contamination.  It’s also rather noisy due to the nearby aircraft, and aircraft exhaust may be an issue.  Site A is located near the Radar/Communication Site, which are potentially hazardous facilities.  There are also munitions storage facilities nearby, representing extremely hazardous operations in the vicinity.
  • You also observe soil discoloration at Site B, some stressed vegetation, and unnatural topography.  The nearby land’s prior use as a tanker field represents potentially hazardous facilities.
  • Site C contains debris piles of scrap metal, and you notice a mild odor from the nearby paper mill.  There is also an existing water treatment facility nearby.
  • At Site D, you observe unnatural topography, stained soils, and two drainage pits.  The site is downwind from the paper mill.  It is also near the munitions storage and somewhat near the flightline.
