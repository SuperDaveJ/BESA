Scenario Information

Based on the sampling results, you determine the HRE for each site.  At Site A, because of the presence of deicing chemicals in the soil, as well as flightline noises above the PEL, you determine the HRE to be High.  At Site B, you estimate a health risk of Moderate due to low levels of fuel contaminants in the soil and in the air.  At Site C, trace levels of TCE are present in the stream but not in the soil, so your HRE for this site is Low.  At Site D, contamination from the former landfill is present in the soil and air.  Because building any facility at Site D would be dangerous due to the explosive nature of landfill gases, you determine an HRE of Extremely High for this location.

With this information, you recommend Site C as the most appropriate site for placement of the new water treatment facility.  After you communicate your initial findings about the health threats to your OIC, CE begins to drill test groundwater wells for the facility from the groundwater reservoirs.  You are requested to conduct sampling for chemical and biological health threats at the test groundwater wells. 

Sampling results indicate that the shallow groundwater reservoir is contaminated with various contaminants (both biological and chemical).  However, the deep groundwater reservoir is free of contaminants, so you determine that the water from the deep groundwater reservoir can be made potable.
