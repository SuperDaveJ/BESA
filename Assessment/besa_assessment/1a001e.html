Scenario Information

Springdale AFB in Windsor, Missouri, has been assigned a new mission that includes flight training for heavy air lift operations.  In order to support this new mission and respond to increased demand for water from the expanding base community, a new water treatment facility and new groundwater wells are scheduled to be built.  The existing water treatment facility is located in an annexed parcel of land and is owned and operated by the neighboring city. Several memoranda of understanding (MOUs) are in place between the base and the city regarding the management and oversight of the existing water treatment facility.

CE has identified four potential sites that are sufficient for drilling groundwater wells and building the water treatment facility from an operational perspective.  They have come to an agreement with the city to maintain the connection with the city water supply.  The connection will remain closed and will be used only as an emergency backup supply.

You have been assigned to the team that will be conducting a Health Risk Assessment (HRA) to identify and analyze health threats and recommend the most appropriate placement of the new facility.

In this scenario-based portion of the Final Exam for the BESA I course, you will answer a series of questions to determine the health threats that exist at each of the prospective sites which could affect base personnel if the facility were built there.
