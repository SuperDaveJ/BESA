Engineering Controls

Engineering controls are methods of isolating or removing hazards from the workplace.  These controls typically target the source, or the reservoir, of the health threat, although at times they target the means of transmission.

Examples of engineering controls that are appropriate to use with biological health threats include disinfection of drinking water and the use of ventilation.

Ventilation is often used in hospitals to control positive and negative pressure relationships.  Maintaining negative pressure in an isolation room keeps air flowing into the room, thus helping to contain the contaminants and prevent their escape into surrounding rooms.  BE is required to periodically check the positive and negative pressure relationships in rooms used for isolating highly contagious patients, as part of the hospital’s infection control program.

Other uses of ventilation include specialized biological safety cabinets and specialized cleaning equipment to remove or inactive biological aerosols.
