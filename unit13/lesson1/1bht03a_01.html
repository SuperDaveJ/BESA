Contact

Infections can be transmitted through direct or indirect contact, including injection and absorption.  Direct contact transmission can occur from person-to-person contact which allows an infectious material to enter a receptive site in the body.  Indirect contact transmission, or person-to-fomite transmission, occurs from touching a contaminated surface.

For example, a person with a cold may spread the illness by coughing into his hand and then shaking another person’s hand (person-to-person transmission) or touching a doorknob that is later used by someone else (person-to-fomite transmission).
