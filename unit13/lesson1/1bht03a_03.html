Inhalation

This type of transmission occurs when infectious particles are released into the air where they can be inhaled into the respiratory system of a host.  Some ways that infectious particles can be released are when a person coughs or sneezes and when contaminated surfaces are disturbed.

Droplet transmission and airborne transmission are two sub-types of inhalation.  Droplet transmission is carried out via relatively large respiratory droplets and travels short, local distances.  Airborne transmission is carried out via smaller respiratory droplets (called droplet nuclei) or virus/bacteria attached to dust particles.  Airborne transmission is capable of traveling longer distances than droplet transmission.
