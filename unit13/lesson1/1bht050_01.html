Bacteria

The oldest and most abundant life forms on earth, bacteria are single-cell organisms that usually need moisture to survive.  Although relatively few bacteria cause infectious disease in humans, the diseases they may cause can be very serious.  Disease-producing bacteria are often found in fecal matter, and the diseases are transmitted by humans.  Bacterial infections can often be successfully treated with antibiotics.

Rickettsia is a genus of non-motile, non-spore forming, and non-encapsulated bacteria which are carried as parasites by ticks, fleas, lice, and mites.
