Direct Noise Pathway

Direct noise is transmitted directly from the source to the receiver.  Examples in which the dominant pathway is direct noise include:

  • Listening to music through headphones.
  • Standing next to a running jet engine.
  • Using a table saw in a shop.

The primary controls used for direct noise sources include:

  • Shields and barriers.
  • Partial enclosures.
  • Total enclosures.
