Noise Pathways

As vital as sound is to daily life, too much of it can be harmful.  In order for a high level of noise to pose a health threat, it must have a source, pathway, and receiver.  If one of these components is eliminated, the threat does not exist.

Most noise producers will usually take two or more paths to the receiver.  There are three primary noise pathways which are determined by how the sound waves are transmitted.
