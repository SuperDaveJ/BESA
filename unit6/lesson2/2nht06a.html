When to Conduct an Octave Band Survey

Once an initial noise source survey has been conducted and the levels are determined to be potentially hazardous, engineering controls should be considered.  At that point, your follow-up actions may include performing an octave band survey, sometimes referred to as an engineering noise survey.

If you find that the 8-hour time weighted average (TWA) is above 94 dB(A), conducting an octave band survey is the preferred course of action.

There are no limits for unprotected noise exposures <80 dB(A).  However, 80 to 85 dB(A) exposures have time-weighted limitations. Exposures of more than 12 hours should be followed by periods of equal length in quiet [less than 72 dB(A)]. Therefore, situations may occur where octave band surveys help contribute to an assessment for finding ways to reduce exposure (e.g., engineering controls).
