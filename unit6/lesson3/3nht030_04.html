The Distance Between the Source and the Receiver

When controlling noise in the path, it’s important to identify the distance from the noise source where the noise levels become constant. This distance, known as the critical distance, helps determine which workers or workstation would benefit by more sound absorbent materials.

Although you will not be calculating the critical distance in this course, you may want to be aware of the formula used to determine it:

dc = √RQ /16 π

Where:
R = room constant
Q = directivity factor
