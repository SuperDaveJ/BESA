Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) Permissible Exposure Levels (PELs)

OSHA standards exist for a number of substances, with specific requirements that apply where these substances are used.  All elements of Air Force exposure prevention programs shall be at least as stringent as those outlined by OSHA.  You can find this information in Title 29 Code of Federal Regulations (CFR) 1910, Subpart Z, Toxic and Hazardous Substances.
