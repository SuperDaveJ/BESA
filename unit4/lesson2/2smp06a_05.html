Personal Sampling

Personal sampling is the most accurate method of evaluating and quantifying a worker’s exposure to airborne chemicals, as it is representative of the worker’s exposure to a contaminant throughout the day.

During personal sampling, the worker wears a sampling device that collects an air sample.  The sampling device is placed as close as possible to the route of entry in the breathing zone of the worker, within 6–9 inches of the nose and mouth, so the data collected closely represents the concentration being inhaled.

Because personal samples are collected over a period of time according to a standard, they can be used for compliance purposes.
