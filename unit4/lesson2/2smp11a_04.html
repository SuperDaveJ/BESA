Short-Term Exposure Limit (OEL-STEL)

The Short-Term Exposure Limit (OEL-STEL) is a concentration to which workers can be exposed continuously for a short period of time without suffering from irritation, chronic or irreversible tissue damage, or debilitating narcosis.  The OEL-STEL represents a 15-minute time-weighted average (TWA) exposure which should not be exceeded at any time during the workday, even if the 8-hour TWA is within OEL-TWA limits.  If the chemical of concern has both an OEL-STEL and an OEL-TWA listed, neither of the two can be exceeded.

The ACGIH TLV book delineates OEL-STELs that allow short exposures to concentrations somewhat higher than allowed by the OEL-TWA.  Exposures within this range should occur less than four times per day, and there should be at least 60 minutes between successive exposures in this range.

Generally, only one sample is included in a 15-minute time period, but when multiple samples must be combined, you use the following formula to calculate a 15-minute TWA:

15-min TWA =

C1T1 + C2T2 + ….CnTn
15 min

Where C = concentration and T = time
