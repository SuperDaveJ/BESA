Site-Specific Risk Assessment

A site-specific risk assessment is a scientific estimation of a human health or ecological hazard required by regulators.  Although BE is not responsible for conducting a site-specific risk assessment, your installation may be required to collect representative samples from the spill site and have the samples analyzed for hazardous substances.  This assessment sampling is done prior to any remediation.

The ultimate outcome of the site-specific risk assessment is the degree of risk, which is then compared to acceptable risk values established by regulators.  The risk assessment identifies the degree of site control needed, cleanup goals, type of treatment, and an estimate of the health threat.
