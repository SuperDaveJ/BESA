Type of Sample (Disturbed or Undisturbed)

Depending on your method, the sample you collect may be disturbed or undisturbed.

A disturbed sample is one whose internal structure has been damaged to such a degree that it does not reasonably approximate that of the material in situ (in its original position).

An undisturbed sample is a sample obtained and handled in such a way that disturbance of its original structure is minimal so that the sample is suitable for laboratory tests of material properties that depend on in situ soil structure.
