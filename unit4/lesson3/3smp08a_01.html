Removing Extraneous Material

Identify and discard materials in a sample which are not relevant or vital for characterizing the sample or the site, since their presence may introduce an error in the sampling or analytical procedures.

Examples of extraneous material in soil samples can include glass pieces, twigs, or leaves.  However, not all non-soil material is extraneous.  Collect samples of any material thought to be a source of contamination for a laboratory extraction procedure.
