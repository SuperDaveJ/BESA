Confidence Limits

The lower confidence limit (LCL) and upper confidence limit (UCL) represent the lowest and the highest value, respectively, that a true exposure could be, within a 95% confidence interval.  You will use sampling and analytical error (SAE) factors to obtain these limits.

Although the SAE can be obtained from OSHA technical manuals or NIOSH, it is ideal to use the values provided by the analyzing lab so you know the exact number they used to correct your sample results and compensate for any errors associated with a particular air sampling method.

The formula to compute the UCL and LCL varies depending on how many samples were taken.
