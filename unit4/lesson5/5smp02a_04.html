Antagonism

Antagonism refers to the interference or inhibition of the effect of one chemical by the action of another chemical.  Therefore, the combined effect of the chemicals is less than the sum of their individual effects.  When antagonism occurs, one contaminant could reduce the effect of the other chemical, or both contaminants could interfere with each other, in effect canceling each other out.  This effect could be represented by the equation, 1+1= 0.
