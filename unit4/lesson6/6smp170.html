Progress Check

BE Tech: Good morning.  I’m with Bioenvironmental Engineering, and I’m looking into possible sources of the lead your children have been exposed to.

Shop Supervisor: Yeah, I got a phone call over the weekend from the Captain letting me know it was okay to take Erica back to the CDC.  She also said you’d be coming by to talk to us.

BE Tech: Good.  I have a few questions for the three of you, if you don’t mind.

Shop Worker 1: No problem.  I don’t know what we can tell you besides what we’ve already told the doctors and nurses, but I’m sure all of us are eager to help however we can so we can figure out what’s going on.

BE Tech: Thank you. Let’s start by considering the kinds of things you are exposed to here in the shop.  Do you ever use any primers or paints that contain lead chromate?

Shop Worker 2: Not usually, but we started using some new primer about a month ago.  In fact, one of the guys from your office came here to check it out.  I’m pretty sure lead chromate was what he was checking for.

Shop Supervisor: Yeah, you’re right.  But I don’t understand what that has to do with our kids.  It’s not like we bring them to work with us.

BE Tech: No, of course not, but we have to consider all the possibilities.  Do you change clothes and wash up thoroughly after work, before going home?

Shop Worker 1: We try to, but the washing machine here has been broken for about two weeks, so we haven’t been able to wash our coveralls here.

BE Tech: So you’ve been washing them at home?

Shop Worker 1: Yeah, is that a problem?

BE Tech: Actually, it is.  The chromates collect on your clothes, so you could be contaminating your house by taking the coveralls home with you. And if you wear them home, your kids can get the chromates on their hands when they hug you.

Shop Worker 2: Wow, I didn’t even think of that.  I’ll make sure I change clothes and wash up before going home from now on.

Shop Supervisor: You bet.  And let’s see what we can do about getting that washer fixed.

On Monday morning, you pay a visit to the corrosion control shop to interview the workers whose children have become ill.
