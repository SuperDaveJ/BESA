Progress Check

As part of the prioritization process, you consider the possible exposure pathways in order to determine their risk ratings, and you decide you need more information.  Your research indicates that many children are poisoned by lead-contaminated dust ingested through normal hand-to-mouth activity.  This dust can come from lead-contaminated soil that is tracked inside on shoes or from the clothes of a parent who works with lead.  Because of the possibility of children playing in contaminated soil or lead dust being tracked inside from the soil, your sampling strategy includes collection of soil samples.

You find that the most common source of lead dust is lead-based paint.  While you know that previous assessments at the CDC facility have not indicated the presence of lead-based paint, painted toys or furniture could have been brought into the center, thereby possibly introducing lead-based paint in the environment.

You remember that lead-based paint presents an immediate lead hazard when it is:

  • Chipping, peeling or flaking.
  • Chalking, thereby producing lead dust.
  • Disturbed by repainting or remodeling.
  • On any surface which is abraded (for example, a floor being walked on or a window being opened and closed).
  • On a surface that can be mouthed by a child (for example, window sills or crib rails).
