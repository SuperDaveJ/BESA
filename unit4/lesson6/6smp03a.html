Progress Check

When inspecting for lead-based paint hazards, you must be careful to evaluate all types of surfaces on the premises, such as the following: 

  • Walls and ceilings.
  • Doors and windows.
  • Trim, jambs, and woodwork.
  • Stairway components.
  • Porch components.
  • Garages and sheds.
  • Painted furniture.
  • Play equipment.
  • Fences.

You already know that lead-based paint was not used on any of the structural components of the CDC facility, so you will focus your sampling efforts mainly on items that may have been introduced into the environment, such as play equipment and furniture.  For surfaces with chipped or peeling paint, you should collect paint chip samples.  For in-tact surfaces, instead of disturbing the paint, you should simply wipe the surface to obtain a sample of the dust.
