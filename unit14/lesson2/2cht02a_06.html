Other Exposure Routes

When selecting RP, you must consider additional exposure routes, most importantly skin absorption.  This is because wearing RP may allow the worker to stay in the area for a longer period of time, thereby putting the worker at greater risk of health effects due to the absorption hazard.

To recognize a skin absorption threat, look for the “skin” designation in the OSHA standards. The “total” designation indicates that the REL or PEL listed is for the “total particulate,” while the “resp” designation refers to the “respirable fraction” of the airborne particulate.
