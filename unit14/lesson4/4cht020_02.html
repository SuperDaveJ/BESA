Static Pressure (SP)

SP exists even where there is no air motion and acts equally in all directions.  In a ventilation system, negative pressure tends to collapse the walls of the duct upstream of the fan, like someone trying to suck a thick milkshake through a straw.  Downstream from the fan, the positive pressure tends to expand the walls of the duct, like someone blowing up a balloon.  A change in static pressure (SP) produces air velocity.
