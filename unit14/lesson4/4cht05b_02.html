Initial Survey

Ideally, an initial test is done before any ventilation system is put into operation to determine whether the system meets design specifications.  By performing the test before the process it has been designed to control has started, you avoid putting workers at risk of being exposed to contaminants if the ventilation system does not work properly.

Systems installed on the basis of air sampling results would require an initial survey.  Systems that have been in place for some time may also need an initial test if they were not correctly evaluated or if they have not been in use.

To determine whether initial survey results are within standards, you will reference your design criteria which are typically from the most current Industrial Ventilation manual.
