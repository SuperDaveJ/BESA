Habitat for Pests and Rodents / Vector Breeding Areas

Pests and rodents can cause a variety of health risks to personnel. Selecting a site that minimizes these risks is important.

You should determine if there are any potential vector-breeding areas nearby such as swamps, ponds, marshy areas, or standing water.

Tall grass and thick underbrush are often the breeding ground for ticks and fleas and should be avoided. Dumps and landfills which attract flies and rodents should also be avoided.

If you discover any of these potential health risks, you should inform Public Health.
