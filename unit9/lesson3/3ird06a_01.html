Written Report

The written report documenting the overexposure must include the:

  • Identity of the person(s) exposed.
  • Circumstances surrounding the exposure.
  • Estimates of the individual’s dose, including how the dose was determined.
  • Portion of the body exposed.
  • Cause of the exposure.
  • Results of medical examination(s).
  • Corrective actions taken.
  • Signed statements supporting or contesting the investigation report.
