Overexposure Investigations

Generally, the following events will trigger suspected overexposure investigations:

  • An individual’s thermoluminescent dosimeter (TLD) results are greater than 500 mR.
  • An individual’s electronic personal dosimeter (EPD) in non-destructive inspection (NDI) situations alarms above 500 mR.
  • A radiographic supervisor suspects someone has been overexposed.
  • A survey meter is returned with readings over the overexposure limit.

When an overexposure occurs in an occupational setting, the individual (or individuals) who have been potentially overexposed will cease all radiographic operations and report the incident to the shop supervisor or the base RSO.

The base RSO will immediately notify the USAFSAM / ESOH Service Center, the MAJCOM BEE, and AFMSA/SG3PB.
