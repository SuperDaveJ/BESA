Bremsstrahlung

Bremsstrahlung x-rays are produced when high-speed electrons interact with a positive nucleus, causing a change in speed and direction.  The result is a loss of kinetic energy in the form of an x-ray. 

Bremsstrahlung is responsible for production of x-rays when shielding high-energy betas with lead.  Since Bremstrahlung x-rays can be produced in this situation, high-energy beta emitters are often shielded with substances such as plastic to avoid additional production of x-rays.
