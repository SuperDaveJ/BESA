ALARA Concept

The ALARA concept should be used when designing or modifying work areas, including determination of the appropriate material and thickness for shielding. BE uses this concept to ensure adequate radiation safety is incorporated into the design plan of new facilities.  Regardless of the standard, you should always try to keep the exposure to personnel as minimal as possible. 

Examples of the use of the ALARA concept include:

  • Installing safety mechanisms (e.g. system interlocks).
  • Controlling access to the exposure room in order to prevent someone from entering the room during an exposure.
  • Posting warning signs.
  • Providing annual training.
  • Issuing appropriate PPE (e.g. lead aprons).
  • Following proper procedures.
  • Performing proper maintenance of the x-ray unit.
  • Workplace / process specific action limits.
