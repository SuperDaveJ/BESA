Step 3

Place a 1-gallon container of water in the beam or the beam’s equivalent and set up your meter.

A 1-gallon container of water is used because it is the normal size of an x-ray beam and water is an inexpensive material that approximates human body tissue.  This creates a situation where the x-rays scatter in the same direction when conducting a survey as during normal operating conditions.

Most meters are auto scaling; however, if the meter has a range selection, you should start at the low range.
