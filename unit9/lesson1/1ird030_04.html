Bremsstrahlung

Bremsstrahlung is an interaction that causes a form of x-ray production in which high-speed beta particles penetrate the electron cloud and interact with the nucleus.

When this occurs, the attraction by the nucleus bends the beta’s path and slows it down.  When this slowing occurs, the beta particle undergoes a loss of kinetic energy.  That loss of energy produces an x-ray.  The energy of this x-ray varies and is dependent upon the amount of kinetic energy lost by the electron. 

Medical departments use this process to generate x-rays for diagnostic purposes.
