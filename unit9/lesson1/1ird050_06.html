Counts per minute (cpm)

The amount of radiation detected by an instrument each minute is expressed in counts per minute (cpm).  Instruments that read in cpm are usually used to detect contamination.  On a typical installation, alpha and beta particles and low level x-rays are measured in the unit of cpm. 

Counts per minute are not equal to disintegrations per minute because the measurement probes are not 100% efficient.  Disintegrations per minute can be estimated by dividing the counts per minute by the efficiency of the probe.

Scintillation probes are commonly used to detect activity.  The amount of activity detected by the probe depends on the efficiency of the tube inside the detection equipment.  Larger tubes will have better efficiency to detect the amount of activity.
