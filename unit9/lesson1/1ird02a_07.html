Radioisotopes

Radioisotopes are unstable isotopes that, in an attempt to become a stable atom, emit energy in the form of radiation.

Different radioisotopes have different uses in industry and the Air Force.

Some examples of radioisotopes in the Air Force include:

  • Cesium-137 (137Cs), used to calibrate radiation detection equipment.
  • Uranium-238 (238U), called depleted Uranium, used in A-10 aircraft ammunition and as counterweights on various aircraft
  • Nickel-63 (63Ni), used in chemical warfare agent detection equipment (e.g., Automatic Chemical Agent Detection Alarm (ACADA)). 

BE personnel must ensure personnel working with these materials do so in a safe fashion.
