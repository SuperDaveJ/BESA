Activity

Activity is the number of disintegrations or transformations of radioactive material per unit of time (usually expressed in seconds).  Activity can be mathematically expressed by using the following equation:

A = λN

Where:
A = activity (expressed in disintegrations per second—dps)
λ = decay constant (seconds-1)
N = number of radioactive atoms decaying
 
You will use the following equation to determine the activity of a given radionuclide at any time,
if you are given an initial activity.

At = Ao e(-λt)

Where:
Ao = original activity (curies or dps*)
e = 2.71828 (base of the natural logarithm system)
At = current activity (curies or dps*)
T1/2 = radiological half-life
t = elapsed time between Ao and At

λ = decay constant simplified to: [ 0.693 ]

*Units must be consistent.
