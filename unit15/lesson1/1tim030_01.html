Example of Catastrophic Effects from TICs/TIMs

One of the worst industrial accidents in history occurred in 1984, when 40 tons of methyl isocyanate gas escaped from a pesticide plant in Bhopal, India, and killed at least 3,000 people within a few days.  Because of long-term health effects caused by the leakage and failure to clean up the toxic remains, many more people have died over the years.  Thousands have also become ill from breathing the toxic air, drinking tainted water, or handling contaminated soil.  The total number of deaths and injuries is unknown.
