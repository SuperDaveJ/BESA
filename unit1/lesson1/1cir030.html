Selectable Elements

Selectable elements have been included in the course to provide additional content and an increased level of interactivity.  For example, whenever you see bold yellow text, you can select it to view more information about the topic. Even though you may know the meaning of a word in bold text, it is important to click on it.  In this course, these popup boxes often contain tables and graphics, and sometimes, selecting a bold word opens a document.

You’ve already seen some examples of selectable graphics.  Throughout the course, you will see other graphics you can click to launch animations, audio, popup text, or example documents.  You will usually be able to identify these selectable graphics by a hexagon shape, as shown below.

It is important that you read the prompt on the bottom of each screen so you don’t miss important information.   During this lesson, while you’re learning, you will see a warning box if you accidentally click Forward without viewing all of the selectable elements on a page.  However, this feature is not continued throughout the rest of the course.

Select the highlighted text and each graphic. Then click Forward to continue.
