Checking Your Understanding

During the course, you will be given continual practice with specific feedback, through the use of two different types of questions that appear throughout the lessons. These questions provide you with a way to monitor your progress.

Appraisals

Appraisals are like mini-quizzes. They are presented after the associated content and are designed to test your understanding and retention of what you’ve learned. The questions are not scored, but you should try your best because they are similar to the questions you’ll see on the Final Exam for the course. You will be able to read feedback about your answers that will help you understand why particular choices are correct or incorrect.

Scenario Challenge Points

Scenario Challenge Point questions are usually presented before the associated content and allow you to challenge yourself to figure out the answers based on your prior knowledge and experience.  You will be able to read content associated with the question, which will be presented directly after the question.

Sometimes you will be asked questions about unfamiliar topics, and the feeling may be uncomfortable.  Unlike most questions you’ve probably encountered in other courses you’ve taken, these questions are not designed to test what you’ve been taught.  Instead, they are designed to start you thinking about the content, and they should help you focus on the information that is ahead.  Keep in mind that these questions are not scored, and most of them allow a second attempt once you’ve read the content if you miss the question the first time.

Like Appraisals, Scenario Challenge Point questions provide valuable practice and specific feedback.
