Lack of Sufficient Oxygen

An atmosphere that lacks sufficient oxygen to support life is called an oxygen-deficient atmosphere.  Any time the oxygen level in a confined space drops below 19.5%, that atmosphere is considered to be oxygen-deficient. 

Oxygen-deficient atmospheres can occur in confined spaces because
oxygen is:

  • Consumed by chemical reactions like oxidation.
  • Displaced by inert gases like argon, carbon dioxide, or nitrogen.
  • Absorbed by porous surfaces such as activated charcoal.
