Excessive Oxygen Levels

An atmosphere that contains excessive oxygen levels (i.e., >23.5%) is called an oxygen-enriched atmosphere.

Oxygen-enriched atmospheres can occur due to poorly designed or malfunctioning oxygen storage and dispensing equipment that allows too much oxygen into the space.  In addition, human error can cause this type of atmosphere if oxygen is used to ventilate a space under the assumption that oxygen and air are one in the same.
