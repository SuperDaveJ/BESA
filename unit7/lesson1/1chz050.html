Considerations for Analyzing a Confined Space

BE has specific responsibilities to ensure the functional manager and/or CC and the CSPT adequately protects the health and safety of entrants into a confined space.

Oxygen, flammability, and toxicity are the three primary considerations addressed when analyzing a confined space with a potentially hazardous atmosphere.  Each consideration is used to determine the proper classification and controls for working in and around the confined space. 

To analyze a confined space you will check for oxygen, flammability, and toxicity using atmospheric monitoring equipment.

DRIs, such as a Combustible Gas Indicator (CGI) or an O2 /LEL meter, are primarily used to monitor the atmosphere of a confined space.
