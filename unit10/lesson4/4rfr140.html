Lesson Summary

You’ve learned that RFR overexposure or accident investigations are conducted whenever a report of a potential overexposure to RFR is made.  The purpose of the investigation is to determine whether the individual was exposed, to what extent, and whether any operation deficiencies exist.

You also learned that, while conducting the investigation, you should conduct interviews and use the information to reconstruct the incident.  Then, you perform calculations to determine if the exposure exceeded the PEL.  Finally, you should make recommendations to prevent future occurrences and file a final report with PH.

In this lesson you:

  • Determined why and when suspected RFR overexposure / accident investigations are performed.
  • Recalled procedures for investigating suspected RFR overexposures / accidents.
