Ensure the Exposed Individual Reports for Medical Screening and Treatment

You should ensure that personnel who were potentially overexposed report for a post-exposure medical examination within 72 hours after the alleged exposure.  Any symptoms which may be associated with the exposure should be reported to the attending physician and recorded for medical investigation purposes.

This examination should include:

  • A standard medical history.
  • A routine medical examination.
  • An eye examination or visual acuity test, if the eyes were involved.
