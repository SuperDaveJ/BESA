Gain

Gain, also referred to as directivity, is the antenna’s ability to concentrate its energy in a certain direction.  The gain is expressed in decibels (dB) and is used to calculate the absolute gain.

Large gains mean the beam is smaller and the energy is more concentrated.  Small gains mean the beam is wider and the energy is spread out further.  Generally, the higher gain antennas are more hazardous due to the concentrated energy.
