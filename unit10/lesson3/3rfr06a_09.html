Absolute Gain (Gabs)

The absolute gain (Gabs) of an antenna, for a given direction and polarization, is the ratio of the power that would be required at the input of an ideal isotropic radiator to the power actually supplied to the given antenna, to produce the same radiant intensity in the far-field region.

The formula used to calculate the absolute gain (Gabs) is:

Gabs = 10(gain/10)
