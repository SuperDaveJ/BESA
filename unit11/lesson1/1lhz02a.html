Operating Principles of Lasers

One form of radiation energy often overlooked in health risk assessments is laser hazards.  The term laser stands for:

 

 

 

 

Laser light is a form of concentrated energy that, with sufficient power and equipment design, can cut through any material.  It travels in a small, tight beam with minimal spreading.  These qualities have resulted in lasers being employed in diverse fields, ranging from military-specific uses to consumer products.
