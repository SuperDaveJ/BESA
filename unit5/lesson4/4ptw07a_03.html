Super-chlorination

Typically during the super-chlorination process, CE adds a chlorine concentration of 50 ppm to the affected section of the system.  After a 24-hour period has passed following super-chlorination, a chlorine residual of at least 25 ppm should remain in the affected area to ensure that proper disinfection is occurring.

If the break occurs in an essential water main, the procedure may be changed to add 300 ppm for a period of 15 minutes, per AWWA C651-05.
