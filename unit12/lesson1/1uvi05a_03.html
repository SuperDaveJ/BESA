UVA

UVA is the long wave type of UV radiation which includes the area on the electromagnetic spectrum between 315 nm and 400 nm.  This type of radiation comes mainly from black lights.

The primary health concerns from this type of UV radiation exposure are skin damage from flash burns, inflammation of the cornea or eyelid, and skin aging.  The symptoms of an overexposure can occur within 30 minutes to 24 hours after exposure.
