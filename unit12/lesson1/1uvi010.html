Lesson Overview

UV/IR are non-ionizing forms of radiation that are used throughout the Air Force for a variety of purposes.  It’s important to be able to identify and analyze UV/IR health threats in order to recommend appropriate controls to adequately protect workers.

While conducting the HRA at the metal shop, you will:

  • Recall UV/IR sources and concerns.
  • Determine UV/IR exposures for a given scenario.
  • Determine appropriate controls for UV/IR radiation.
  • Select appropriate UV/IR PPE, given hazards and equipment limitations.
  • Determine if PPE for UV/IR is used and maintained in accordance with established guidelines.

This lesson should take you approximately 15 minutes to complete.

Narrator:  During the morning meeting with the rest of the BE Techs in your shop, you volunteer to perform a routine HRA at a metal shop on base to ensure that newly issued hearing protection is working properly.  While you’re there, you will also investigate any issues the shop supervisor may bring to your attention.
